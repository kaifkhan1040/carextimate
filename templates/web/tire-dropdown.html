TYRE SIZES EXPLAINED

When it comes to tyres, understanding the various specifications is crucial for optimal performance, safety, and fuel efficiency. In this guide, we'll break down the key components of tyre specifications, helping you make informed decisions when purchasing new tyres .

Width : The tyre's width is represented by the first three digits on the sidewall. A wider tyre provides better grip, but may increase road noise.

Profile (Aspect Ratio) : The aspect ratio is calculated by dividing the sidewall height by the tyre width and multiplying by 100. This ratio affects the tyre's performance and handling.

Rim : The rim size is the diameter of the wheel rim, which may differ slightly from the tyre width. Both dimensions are provided separately for accurate fitting.

Speed Rating : The speed rating indicates the maximum speed at which the tyre can safely carry its load. Ratings range from Q (up to 99 mph) to Y (over 186 mph).

Load Index : The load index represents the maximum weight the tyre can carry at the specified speed. Ensure the load index matches your vehicle's requirements.

Extra Load (XL) : Some tyres are designed for heavier loads and higher inflation pressures, marked as Extra Load (XL) or Reinforced (RF).

Runflat Tyres : Runflat tyres feature reinforced sidewalls that support the vehicle's weight after a puncture, allowing for limited driving at moderate speeds.This ‘Self-Supporting’ Tyre Technology is typically found on BMWs, Minis,Porsche and enables you to continue your journey with a flat tyre for up to 40-50 miles at up to 50 miles an hour.
